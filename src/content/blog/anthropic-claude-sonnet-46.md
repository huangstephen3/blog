Anthropic's [Introducing Sonnet 4.6](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-sonnet-4-6) announcement on February 18, 2026 is a product story with real workflow consequences.

## What happened

Anthropic said **Claude Sonnet 4.6** is now the default model in **claude.ai** and **Claude Cowork** for Free and Pro users. The company also highlighted a **1 million token context window in beta**, while keeping pricing aligned with the prior Sonnet tier.

The companion product page for [Claude Sonnet 4.6](https://www.anthropic.com/claude/sonnet) frames the release around hybrid reasoning and agent-ready usage rather than a single narrow benchmark.

## Why it matters

This release matters because it pushes a feature that used to feel premium or experimental into the default user experience.

- A **1M token context window** changes how teams think about code review, contract analysis, and research synthesis
- Making the model **default** lowers adoption friction inside existing Claude workflows
- Holding the price line suggests Anthropic wants wider usage, not just a prestige launch

If OpenAI's February news was about scale economics, Anthropic's move is about making long-horizon work feel normal. That is an important shift for agents and for knowledge-heavy enterprise tasks.

## Editorial take

The interesting signal is not only that Anthropic can support bigger context. It is that the company is betting people will actually use it if the friction stays low enough. Large context windows only matter when they are packaged into a product people already trust for everyday work.
